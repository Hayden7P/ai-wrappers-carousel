Dependency economics 5 / 11

The Black Mirror effect in AI pricing.

Ref: "Joan Is Awful" (S6E1) — trapped in a platform's terms; the cost to exit rises as dependency deepens

01
You sign up. You agreed to the terms.
Like Joan discovering her life is being streamed by Streamberry — she agreed somewhere in the ToS. You integrated the model without reading the pricing footnotes.
↳ The value is real. The contract is buried.
02
You can't opt out. Your workflows already depend on it.
Every agent, prompt chain, and team process runs through the platform. Switching means rebuilding from zero. The model knows your codebase. You don't want to start over.
↳ Opting out costs more than staying in.
03
Each upgrade raises the floor — and your dependency.
Models improve. Limits tighten. API costs rise with every new flagship. You can't go back to the dumber model; it no longer handles your use cases. The escalator only goes up.
↳ Not a trap. A ratchet. Each step is rational. The direction is fixed.

What gets compressed
Horizontal UI wrappersA prettier chat interface on top of Claude isn't a product. Claude Cowork already is one.

Infrastructure & compliancen8n: open-source, self-hosted, 700K+ devs. A different buyer, a different contract.

The real divide: companies that own a workflow, a dataset, or a compliance layer vs. companies that rent access to one and resell it with a UI.

Moat analysis 9 / 11

Four durable moats in a world where the model is a commodity.

Moat 01
Workflow Ownership
You own a critical step in a business process — not just an AI call. The product has non-AI logic, data, and integrations Claude can't replicate natively.
→ Glean, Sierra, Replit
Moat 02
Own Model Layer
You build or fine-tune your own models. You're not a wrapper — you're a lab. Anthropic expanding doesn't touch your core differentiation.
→ Poolside, Cognition
Moat 03
Community & Distribution
Self-hosted, open-source, or community-driven. Your users have deployment control. You're not competing on the model — you're competing on trust and ownership.
→ n8n (700K+ devs)
Moat 04
IDE & Context Lock-in
You sit inside the developer's environment and accumulate codebase-specific knowledge over time. Switching means retraining the tool from zero.
→ Cursor (1M+ DAU)

Investor thesis 11 / 11

This is not SaaS vs. agentic.
It's about which layer you own.

01
The platform expansion of Claude compresses thin wrappers but validates deep ones. $14B+ in fundraising proves the market is real — the question is who captures the margin.
02
The Black Mirror effect is a structural feature, not a bug. Every company integrating Claude is trading switching cost for short-term productivity — and that dependency reprices with every model upgrade.
03
Alpha in this market comes from identifying which wrappers own a workflow, a dataset, or an enterprise relationship that Anthropic can't replicate with a product update — or spotting the acquisition targets before the platform moves.
04
The wrapper era isn't ending. It's stratifying. Category-defining tools become platforms. Generic ones become features. The race is to move up the stack before Claude arrives at your layer.
Anthropic isn't replacing startups — it's forcing every AI company to articulate what they actually own. That's a healthy pressure. The survivors will be worth significantly more for having answered it.
